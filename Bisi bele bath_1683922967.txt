Ingredients:
- 1 cup short grain rice
- 1/2 cup toor dal
- 2 carrots, chopped
- 1 cup green beans, chopped
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 1 medium tomato, chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
- 1/4 cup peanuts
- 2 tablespoons ghee
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 2 dried red chilies
- 1/2 tablespoon mustard seeds
- Pinch of hing
- Salt to taste
- Water

For the masala powder:
- 2 tablespoons coriander seeds
- 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
- 1/2 tablespoon black peppercorns
- 1/2 tablespoon fenugreek seeds
- 1/2 tablespoon mustard seeds
- 1/4 cup chana dal
- 1/4 cup urad dal
- 5-6 dried red chilies
- 1/4 cup curry leaves
- 1/2 tablespoon cinnamon powder

Instructions:

1. In a pan, dry roast the ingredients for masala powder until lightly browned.

2. Allow the roasted ingredients to cool down completely and then grind them into a fine powder.

3. Rinse the rice and toor dal in water until water runs clear.

4. Add the rice, toor dal, chopped carrots, green beans, chopped onion, chopped tomato, turmeric, tamarind paste, peanuts, and salt in a pressure cooker.

5. Add about 4 cups of water to the pressure cooker and mix everything well.

6. Close the lid and cook for 3-4 whistles or until the rice, dal, and vegetables are fully cooked.

7. Heat a pan with ghee and add cumin seeds, dried red chilies, mustard seeds, and pinch of hing.

8. Once the mustard seeds start popping, reduce the heat and add the ground masala powder to the pan.

9. Fry the masala powder on low heat until it releases its aroma and turns slightly brown.

10. Once the rice and dal mixture is cooked, slowly add the masala powder to the pressure cooker and mix everything well.

11. Let the Bisi Bela Bath simmer for a few minutes on low heat until the mixture thickens.

12. Serve the Bisi Bela Bath hot with papad and pickle.

Enjoy your delicious Bisi Bela Bath Indian meal!
